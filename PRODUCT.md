# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One employee on one Mac, working a full shift producing personalized children’s picture books. Context: WhatsApp with parents on one screen, Hekayati in the browser on another. Jobs: intake photos and consent, build character library, generate sheets and stories, review pages, send watermarked previews, export print PDFs. Speed, clarity, and trust matter more than marketing flair. The customer never uses the app.

## Product Purpose

Hekayati (حكايتي) is a local desktop-operated web app that turns a child into the hero of a printed Arabic picture book. Success looks like: from customer photos to print-ready interior + cover PDFs in one working day, with durable jobs, human review gates, and no silent AI fallbacks. The product is a gift first; any developmental goal stays subtle and dignified.

## Brand Personality

Playful, sunlit, gift-ready.

Voice: simple Modern Standard Arabic in the UI; warm, age-appropriate Egyptian Arabic in stories. The brand should feel like a citrus playground birthday afternoon: cheerful, clear, never preachy, never corporate-purple “AI kids app.”

## Anti-references

- Purple-on-white / indigo SaaS gradients
- Generic cream + terracotta + serif “AI editorial” kits
- Dark neon “tooling” dashboards for their own sake
- Inter / Roboto / Arial as primary brand faces
- Glassmorphism, glow stacks, pill forests, emoji as decoration
- Cartoon chaos that fights Arabic readability or print seriousness
- Anything that looks like a customer self-serve portal (out of scope)

## Design Principles

1. **Operator first.** Every screen serves a production task; decoration never blocks scanning, queues, or errors.
2. **Gift energy, workshop clarity.** Brand citrus shows on identity moments (logo, ending page, watermark, empty states); the working chrome stays calm and readable.
3. **Arabic is the product.** RTL, shaping, and Egyptian story tone are requirements, not themes.
4. **Honest AI.** No silent provider switches, no fake exact likeness, no shame framing in copy or stories.
5. **Print is the finish line.** UI choices must survive WhatsApp previews and hardcover A4 output.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.2 AA for operator UI contrast and focus
- Full RTL layout; Arabic UI copy; story text Egyptian Arabic
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Do not rely on color alone for job state, errors, or consent warnings
- Touch targets and keyboard paths usable at 1440×900+
- Child-photo privacy: never display secrets; minimize what hits the screen in screenshots
